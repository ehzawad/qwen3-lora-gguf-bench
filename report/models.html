Model choice at 4-bit: 4B vs 9B vs an "E2B"

On the same 24 GB GPU, how do a bigger model (Qwen3.5-9B) and a smaller efficiency model (Gemma-4-E2B) compare to our 4B? The punchline: serving footprint tracks architecture, not parameter count — the bigger model can need less VRAM.

Read this first — the fairness rulesMatched nominal Q4_K_M tier, NOT iso-precision. Identical quantize commands produced different bits-per-weight — llama.cpp keeps far more of Gemma's tensors at F32/Q6_K (its per-layer-embedding machinery), so Gemma's "Q4_K_M" is effectively ~5.9-bit, the densest of the three. This is speed & footprint only, not quality (different tokenizers → tok/s ≠ equal work; no cross-model perplexity), and text-only (both new models are multimodal). Qwen3.5-9B is dense, not MoE. No "inherently faster/leaner" claims — every number combines architecture + quant recipe + kernels.

Gemma leads even though its quant is the densest (5.9 bpw) — its "E2B ≈ 2B effective" design is a plausible reason (fewer params active per token) — this benchmark shows the speed, not the mechanism in isolation. Qwen3.5-9B (8.95B served text weights) decodes at ~half the 4B's rate and prefills ~2× slower: the honest cost of the bigger model.

2 · Parameter count does not predict footprint

Measured static VRAM with a 64-slot server (ground truth) + the attention design from GGUF metadata.
ModelWeightsStatic VRAM @ 64 slotsAttention design
Gemma-4-E2B3.43 GB3,224 MiB (3.15 GiB)sliding-window 512 + 20 shared-KV layers, MQA
Qwen3.5-9B5.63 GB10,942 MiB (10.69 GiB)hybrid: full attn every 4th layer (8/32) + 24 SSM/recurrent
Qwen3-4B2.50 GB12,208 MiB (11.92 GiB)uniform GQA → 144 KiB/token
The counterintuitive resultThe 8.95B-text Qwen3.5-9B uses LESS VRAM at 64 slots than our 4B (10.69 vs 11.92 GiB) — but not from GQA as I first guessed: its metadata shows a hybrid design where only 8 of 32 layers do full attention and the rest are SSM/recurrent (fixed per-sequence state, not per-token KV). Gemma is leanest (3.15 GiB): mostly sliding-window attention with KV shared across 20 layers + MQA, so KV barely grows with context. Only the 4B's 144 KiB/token fits the simple formula. Parameter count didn't predict VRAM here — modern KV-saving architectures (recurrent, sliding-window, shared-KV) did.

Gemma and the 4B track each other closely; Qwen3.5-9B runs at ~45–50% of their throughput throughout — a consistent gap (combining its larger served-weight size, hybrid architecture, quant recipe, and kernels — not size alone). On one A5000 you either serve the fast small/efficient models at high concurrency, or the bigger Qwen3.5-9B at roughly half the aggregate rate — a direct model-choice/throughput trade you now have numbers for (this measured throughput, not capability/quality).

CaveatsThe bpw differences (Gemma 5.9 vs ~5.0) make this a matched-tier, not equal-precision, comparison. TTFT was captured only for the 4B (a streaming-format quirk left Qwen3.5/Gemma TTFT unrecorded — throughput/VRAM unaffected). All runs are llama.cpp only: vLLM 0.11.0 predates qwen35/gemma4, so a multi-engine take on the new models is a separate follow-up. Text-only, one GPU, one quant tier.
