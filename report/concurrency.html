Concurrency capacity — where it peaks, and what breaks first

Raise concurrent streams from 1 to 128 on one 24 GB GPU. Throughput doesn't rise forever — it peaks near C=64 and declines, because the limiter leaves the GPU.

Method. llama.cpp, closed-loop barrier-synchronous load, ignore_eos, ~242-token prompts / exactly 256 generated, --parallel == C, 768-token slots, f16 KV, --no-kv-unified. A saturation-capacity probe — not a real arrival-pattern test. Per-worker sample counts scale down with C (60 at C=1 → 10 at C≥16); a C=32 repeat and an independent Work-A run cross-check the curve.

What breaks firstNot VRAM (14.6 GB of 24 at C=100), not thermal (≤78 °C). As C rises the GPU is underfed (util/power fall, p95 pins 99% = bursty gaps) while server-process CPU rises ~11×. The evidence most strongly points to a host-side feed/orchestration bottleneck (CPU-scheduling the leading candidate) — but no profiler was run, so the exact mechanism isn't isolated. Practical rule (this rig): operate C≈24–32 for balance, ≤4 for latency, ~48–64 for max throughput; don't exceed ~64.
